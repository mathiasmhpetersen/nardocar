## Idea 1: "Midnight Garage" - Dark Industrial Automotive

**Design Movement:** Neo-Industrial / Dark Automotive Luxury. Inspired by high-end car photography studios and late-night garage culture. Think moody, dramatic lighting against dark surfaces.

**Core Principles:**
1. Dramatic contrast between deep blacks and sharp accent highlights
2. Photography-first layouts where images dominate and text is secondary
3. Cinematic pacing - each scroll reveals a new "scene"
4. Tactile textures - brushed metal, concrete, carbon fiber patterns

**Color Philosophy:** A near-black base (oklch 0.12) with warm amber/gold accents (oklch 0.78 0.15 75) representing garage light. White text for readability. The palette evokes the feeling of a premium garage at midnight - focused, intimate, purposeful.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed vertical storytelling. Each section is a full-viewport "frame" like a film reel. Asymmetric image-text splits with images taking 60-70% of the viewport. No traditional grid - content flows organically like a documentary.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Horizontal light-streak dividers between sections (simulating headlight trails)
2. Subtle grain/noise texture overlay on all backgrounds
3. "Spotlight" effect on hover - elements illuminate as if a garage light swings over them

**Interaction Philosophy:** Slow, deliberate reveals. Content fades and slides in as you scroll, mimicking the experience of walking through a dimly lit showroom where each car is revealed one at a time.

**Animation:** Parallax depth on hero images. Text slides in from the left with a slight delay. Section transitions use a subtle "curtain" wipe. Hover states on CTAs glow with the amber accent.

**Typography System:** Display: "Oswald" (condensed, uppercase for headlines - industrial strength). Body: "Source Sans 3" (clean, readable, slightly technical). Hierarchy: Massive display headlines (clamp 3rem-6rem), small uppercase labels, generous line-height body text.

## Idea 2: "Raw Motion" - Kinetic Brutalist Automotive

**Design Movement:** Digital Brutalism meets Automotive Culture. Raw, unapologetic, high-energy. Inspired by car meet flyers, racing liveries, and underground car culture zines.

**Core Principles:**
1. Bold, oversized typography that dominates the viewport
2. Exposed structure - visible grid lines, raw edges, no polish
3. High-contrast color blocking with intentional "clashing"
4. Content hierarchy through scale, not decoration

**Color Philosophy:** Pure white (#FAFAFA) and jet black (#0A0A0A) as the primary pair, with a single electric accent - vivid red-orange (oklch 0.65 0.25 30) representing brake heat, exhaust glow, and urgency. The palette is deliberately restricted to create maximum impact.

**Layout Paradigm:** Broken grid with overlapping elements. Text bleeds off-screen. Images are cropped aggressively - you never see the full car, just the most compelling detail. Sections stack with intentional overlap, creating depth through layering rather than spacing.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Oversized numbers/statistics that break out of their containers
2. Diagonal cuts and angled section dividers (like racing stripes)
3. Monospace "data labels" next to key metrics (like a spec sheet)

**Interaction Philosophy:** Aggressive and responsive. Elements react immediately to cursor movement. Hover states are dramatic - scale changes, color inversions. The page feels alive and reactive, like a car responding to throttle input.

**Animation:** Text reveals with a "typewriter" stagger effect. Images slide in with momentum (overshoot + settle). Scroll-triggered counter animations for statistics. Page transitions use a hard horizontal wipe.

**Typography System:** Display: "Space Grotesk" (geometric, modern, slightly technical). Body: "JetBrains Mono" for labels and data, "Space Grotesk" regular for body. Hierarchy: Enormous display text (clamp 4rem-8rem), tight letter-spacing, all-caps for section headers.

## Idea 3: "Nordic Workshop" - Scandinavian Precision Craft

**Design Movement:** Scandinavian Functionalism meets Workshop Aesthetic. Clean, precise, warm. Inspired by Danish design principles - where form follows function but beauty is never sacrificed. Think Nardocar's Danish roots meeting premium craftsmanship.

**Core Principles:**
1. Warm minimalism - clean but never cold or sterile
2. Precision in every detail - pixel-perfect alignment, intentional spacing
3. Material honesty - textures that reference real workshop materials (wood, leather, steel)
4. Content-first hierarchy with generous breathing room

**Color Philosophy:** Warm off-white base (oklch 0.97 0.01 80) like sanded birch wood. Deep charcoal text (oklch 0.20 0.01 60). Accent: a muted teal-green (oklch 0.55 0.12 175) representing precision and trust - the color of quality inspection marks. Secondary warm tone (oklch 0.85 0.04 70) for subtle backgrounds.

**Layout Paradigm:** Modular card-based layout with generous margins. Content is organized in clear "workstation" blocks - each section is a self-contained module with clear purpose. Asymmetric two-column layouts where text and image alternate sides. Generous vertical rhythm (8px base unit, scaled up).

**Signature Elements:**
1. Thin horizontal rules with small "craft marks" (dots or crosses) at intersections
2. Subtle paper/linen texture on card backgrounds
3. Small illustrative icons in a hand-drawn line style for features/benefits

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth and considered. Nothing jumps or startles. Hover states are gentle - a slight lift, a color shift. Scrolling feels like turning pages in a well-crafted catalog. Every interaction confirms quality.

**Animation:** Gentle fade-up reveals on scroll (translateY 20px, opacity 0→1, 600ms ease-out). Cards lift slightly on hover with a soft shadow expansion. Page load uses a staggered entrance - elements appear in reading order. No bouncing, no overshooting.

**Typography System:** Display: "DM Serif Display" (elegant serif with Danish character). Body: "DM Sans" (clean geometric sans that pairs perfectly). Hierarchy: Refined display headlines (clamp 2.5rem-4.5rem), generous letter-spacing on labels, comfortable 1.7 line-height for body text.
